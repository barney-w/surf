# Leave Policy

**Document Reference:** HR-POL-2024-003
**Effective Date:** 1 July 2024
**Review Date:** 30 June 2027
**Source:** Enterprise Agreement 2024

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the leave entitlements available to all employees in accordance with the Enterprise Agreement 2024, the *Fair Work Act 2009* (Cth), and applicable legislation.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all permanent full-time and part-time employees. Casual employees are entitled to unpaid leave as provided under the National Employment Standards.

---

## 3. Annual Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement

- Full-time employees are entitled to **20 days (4 weeks)** of paid annual leave per year.
- Shift workers are entitled to **25 days (5 weeks)** of paid annual leave per year.
- Part-time employees accrue annual leave on a pro-rata basis.

### 3.2 Accrual

Annual leave accrues progressively during each year of service according to the employee's ordinary hours of work. Leave begins accruing from the first day of employment.

### 3.3 Taking Annual Leave

- Annual leave must be approved by the employee's direct manager.
- Requests should be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance for periods of 5 days or less, and 4 weeks in advance for longer periods.
- The organisation may direct an employee to take annual leave where the accrued balance exceeds 40 days.

### 3.4 Payment

Annual leave is paid at the employee's base rate of pay. A leave loading of **17.5%** is payable on annual leave.

---

## 4. Personal / Carer's Leave (Sick Leave)

### 4.1 Entitlement

- Full-time employees are entitled to **10 days** of paid personal/carer's leave per year.
- Part-time employees accrue personal leave on a pro-rata basis.
- Unused personal leave accumulates from year to year without limit.

### 4.2 Use of Personal Leave

Personal leave may be used when the employee is:
- Not fit for work due to personal illness or injury; or
- Required to provide care or support to an immediate family member or household member due to illness, injury, or unexpected emergency.

### 4.3 Evidence Requirements

- For absences of **1 or 2 days**, a statutory declaration or medical certificate may be requested.
- For absences **exceeding 2 consecutive days**, a medical certificate is required.
- The organisation reserves the right to request evidence for any absence where a pattern of concern is identified.

---

## 5. Long Service Leave

### 5.1 Entitlement

- Employees are entitled to **13 weeks (65 business days)** of long service leave after **10 years** of continuous service.
- After the initial 10 years, additional long service leave accrues at the rate of 1.3 weeks per year of service.

### 5.2 Pro-Rata Access

- Pro-rata long service leave may be accessed after **7 years** of continuous service in certain circumstances, including:
  - Serious illness of the employee or immediate family member
  - Redundancy or retrenchment

### 5.3 Payment

Long service leave is paid at the employee's ordinary rate of pay at the time leave is taken.

---

## 6. Parental Leave

### 6.1 Eligibility

Employees who have completed at least **12 months** of continuous service are eligible for parental leave.

### 6.2 Entitlement

- **Primary carer:** Up to **14 weeks** of paid parental leave, plus up to **38 weeks** of unpaid parental leave (total of up to **12 months**).
- **Secondary carer:** Up to **2 weeks** of paid partner leave within the first 12 months after birth or placement.
- An employee may request an extension of up to an additional **12 months** of unpaid parental leave (total of up to 24 months).

### 6.3 Application Process

1. Submit a **Parental Leave Application Form** to your manager at least **10 weeks** before the expected date of birth or adoption placement.
2. Provide a medical certificate or statutory declaration confirming the expected date.
3. Discuss your intended leave period and return-to-work arrangements with your manager.
4. HR will confirm your leave entitlements and any applicable paid leave in writing.

### 6.4 Keeping-in-Touch Days

Employees on parental leave may work up to 10 "keeping-in-touch" days without affecting their leave entitlements.

---

## 7. Compassionate / Bereavement Leave

- Employees are entitled to **3 days** of paid compassionate leave per occasion for the death or serious illness of an immediate family member or household member.
- Casual employees are entitled to 3 days of unpaid compassionate leave.

---

## 8. Community Service Leave

Employees are entitled to unpaid leave for:
- **Jury duty** — with make-up pay (the difference between jury duty fees and ordinary pay).
- **Voluntary emergency management activities** — reasonable unpaid leave as required.

---

## 9. Domestic and Family Violence Leave

- Employees (including casuals) are entitled to **10 days** of paid domestic and family violence leave per year.
- This leave does not accumulate and is available in full at the start of each year.
- All requests are treated with strict confidentiality.

---

## 10. Leave Without Pay

- Leave without pay may be granted at the discretion of the relevant Director.
- Periods of unpaid leave exceeding 5 days do not count as service for leave accrual purposes.

---

## 11. Public Holidays

- Employees are entitled to paid leave on all gazetted public holidays.
- Where an employee is required to work on a public holiday, penalty rates apply as per the Enterprise Agreement.
